Overviews

Overview of: Jo math
Jo math, by far the oldest of all the maths (except for Real math, or RM in Jo Math abbreviations), made its first appearence in a document when I (Moses) was 4 or 5. It started out with a few simple variables (A = 3, X = 7, and C = 6), and evolved. Today there are many different variables, and very many different fields of study. Perhaps one of the most interesting things about Jo math is that today there is a field of study actually called "Jo math Boppy"!
See more on Jo math on The history and origin of Jo math

Overview of: Dragon math
Dragon math, which is the closest to Real math of all the maths invented by me (Moses), is really Real math, just where certain things that were decided by the real math community where I did not agree were changed (for example, it was agreed by the real math community that 0 factorial equals 1, but I did not agree with that, so I changed it to 0 factorial equals 0, because it just seems weird that it is 1.
See more on Dragon math on The history and origin of Dragon math

Overview of: Baby math
Baby math, is, actually, as close as algoritims can get to the math that babies do without getting to complex (2 + 5 = 25). Since I (Moses) started it when I was six, (I think), I still remembered some of the silly things that I used to say when I was a baby, at least remembering them better than I do now. I stored all that in baby math, and now, baby math is quite an important math in studing base changes.

Overview of: Picture math
Picture math, problaby the most fun math (besides Real Math), was actually not invented by me (Moses). Instead, it was invented by Sean Johnson. Picture math involves drawing pictures (fun!), for certain things turning that drawing into something you can make with a piece of string (fun!) and studing the configurations of drawings.
See more on Picture math on The history and origin of Picture math

Overview of: Boppy
Boppy is a concept my brother once said. Sometimes, back when he first said boppy, I could say a real word, and then he would say that word back to me with his pronuciation, and then I found out what we meant. But with boppy, both brown and broccoli in hilge's pronuciation sounded like boppy. So I made it a consept, which could mean anything. At that time, it then represented an emotion that you didn’t want to say, like “I’m feeling boppy.” If you were using boppy to exchange for any emotion and you still wanted to make it kinda visible how you were feeling, then you would add a tone that matches with the emotion. That meaning is still used some of the time today, but the more commonly used meaning today is where you use boppy to show what you or someone else is doing, like “I must be doing something boppy”.
